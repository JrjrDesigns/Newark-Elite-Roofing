Know the Signs

When Does a Licking County Roof Need to Be Replaced?

A roof repair makes sense when the damage is isolated and the rest of the roof has years of life left. A full replacement makes sense when the roof itself is the problem — not just one section of it.

We hear this a lot: "Can't you just patch it?" Our answer is usually yes — if the roof is under 15 years old and the damage is contained to one area. But in our experience with Licking County homes, once a roof starts failing in multiple spots, patching becomes an expensive way to delay the inevitable. We'll always tell you honestly which situation you're in.
  • Age 20+ years — most asphalt shingle roofs in Ohio are designed for 25–30 years, but the Licking County climate accelerates wear
  • Widespread granule loss — bare spots across multiple sections, not just one area
  • Multiple active leaks — more than one water entry point means the system has failed
  • Curling, cracking, or missing shingles across large portions of the roof
  • Sagging roof deck — structural sign that water has been getting in for a long time
  • Significant hail or storm damage — widespread impact damage accelerates shingle failure even when not immediately visible

Our Standard

Installed Right the First Time

The difference between a roof that lasts 20 years and one that starts failing in 12 comes down to installation — nail placement, shingle alignment, flashing technique, and how penetrations are sealed.

Every replacement Newark Elite Roofing performs follows the manufacturer's installation requirements exactly. That matters because improper installation voids the shingle warranty — something that often only comes to light when a homeowner needs to make a claim.

When customers ask us what separates a good roof from a mediocre one, we usually point to the flashing. Anyone can put shingles on a flat section. Where roofs fail first is almost always at transitions — chimneys, skylights, valleys, and wall intersections. We use step flashing at every wall junction, not caulk.

We also provide a written walkthrough at the end of every job. We'll show you the ridge cap, the flashing points, and anywhere we found deck damage during tearoff — so you have a complete picture of what was done and where.

Material Selection

Choosing the Right Shingle for Your Newark Home

Color and material choice matters more than most homeowners realize — both for the look of the home and its long-term performance in Licking County's climate.

Architectural (dimensional) shingles are the standard we install on most homes. They offer a meaningful upgrade over three-tab shingles in thickness, wind resistance, and appearance — and they carry longer warranties.

We see a lot of this: Homeowners who chose the cheapest shingle option during their last replacement and are back 12 years later. Ohio's hail exposure and freeze-thaw cycles are hard on budget shingles. We always walk through material options honestly — the price difference between standard and impact-resistant shingles is often smaller than people expect, and the performance difference is significant.
  • Charcoal / Onyx Black — Most popular in Licking County. Pairs with white, gray, and beige siding.
  • Weathered Wood — Warm neutral. Popular on brick homes throughout Newark and Heath.
  • Brownwood / Driftwood — Earthy tones that work well with the older housing stock in Granville.
  • Slate Blue / Colonial Slate — Bold choice that's gaining popularity on newer Pataskala and Johnstown builds.
