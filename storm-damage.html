Know What to Look For

Hail Damage Isn't Always Visible from the Ground

After a hailstorm moves through Licking County, most homeowners walk their yard, look up at their roof, and conclude everything looks fine. In many cases, they're wrong — and so begins a slow deterioration that ends in a much bigger problem a few years later.

After a significant hail event in central Ohio we were on over twenty roofs across Newark, Heath, and Pataskala in a four-day span. In the majority of cases, the homeowner had no idea damage had occurred — and in several cases their neighbors had already filed insurance claims and were getting new roofs. Hail impacts on asphalt shingles are often smaller than a quarter and only visible from six feet away on the roof surface. You cannot assess this from the street.
  • Circular impact craters on shingles where granules have been knocked off
  • Dented gutters and downspouts — if the metal is dented, the shingles took hits too
  • Granules in gutters — a large granule deposit after a storm indicates significant shingle impact
  • Missing shingles — wind events can lift and remove shingles entirely
  • Damaged window screens or HVAC units — if soft metals are dented, your roof was hit
Immediate Protection

Emergency Tarping — Stop the Water Now

If your roof has exposed decking, large missing sections, or active water intrusion, the first priority is stopping further damage to your home's interior — not waiting days for a permanent repair appointment.

Newark Elite Roofing can deploy heavy-duty emergency tarping the same day you call. We secure tarps over the affected area to prevent additional water from entering while a complete repair or replacement is scheduled.

When customers call us after a severe storm and describe active dripping or exposed decking, we always say the same thing: get tarped first, assess second. Every hour of open exposure is water working its way deeper into the insulation, decking, and ceiling structure. A tarp deployed the same day protects your home and protects your insurance claim — it demonstrates you took reasonable steps to prevent further damage, which matters during the claims process.
